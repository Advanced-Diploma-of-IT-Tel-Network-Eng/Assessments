Dear Lucas,

I am writing to provide you with a formal update on the current performance of Team Worlducation I+D and to report three internal issues that have been identified as direct risks to the successful delivery of the Cloud-First SaaS & B2C project.

## Background

Following the implementation of our Team Performance Plan and the first two sprint cycles, a structured review of team dynamics and performance revealed three issues that, if left unaddressed, could impact our ability to meet project milestones and ultimately affect the organisation's transition timeline and profitability targets.

## Issues Identified

### Issue 1 - Poor Information Sharing and Work Duplication

During sprint reviews, it became evident that team members were not consistently sharing progress updates, resulting in duplication of work between the cloud infrastructure and platform testing workstreams. This directly risks delaying the SaaS platform go-live date of 31 October 2021 and increasing project costs through inefficient use of resources.

### Issue 2 - Inappropriate Internet Use

It was identified that a team member was using the internet for personal purposes during work hours. Beyond the immediate productivity impact, this behaviour poses a cybersecurity risk to the project, particularly given the sensitivity of the cloud infrastructure and data privacy obligations under the **Privacy Act 1988** and **Cybercrime Act 2001**.

### Issue 3 - Resistance to Cloud Technology Adoption

More experienced team members expressed reluctance to fully adopt cloud-based technologies, citing concerns about enterprise maturity. This resistance is a direct risk to the Cloud-First SaaS & B2C project, as it affects sprint velocity, team alignment, and the organisation's ability to deliver the solution within the approved timeline and budget.

## Solutions Implemented

I am pleased to report that all three issues have been actively addressed using the following strategies:

**Internet Use Policy (IT_Policy_InternetUsage_v1.0)**
A formal Internet Use Policy has been developed and communicated to the entire team. The policy is aligned with Australian standards including the Privacy Act 1988, the Cybercrime Act 2001, and the ACSC guidelines. It establishes clear boundaries for acceptable internet use, consequences for breaches, and monitoring protocols. A copy is attached for your formal approval.

**Nominal Group Technique (NGT) - Concern Resolution Process**
A structured concern resolution process was implemented using the Nominal Group Technique. This approach ensured all team members contributed equally to identifying and voting on solutions, reducing the influence of dominant voices and building genuine consensus. The process has been formalised as the team's standard approach for addressing concerns going forward.

**Collaborative Tools - Scrum Framework and Trello**
The team is currently operating on two-week sprint cycles managed through Trello. This provides real-time visibility of all tasks, clear ownership, and a shared accountability framework. Sprint retrospectives and daily standups have created regular, structured forums for open communication, early issue identification, and continuous improvement.

**Knowledge-Sharing Session on Cloud Technology**
To address resistance to cloud adoption, a dedicated knowledge-sharing session was facilitated where enterprise cloud deployment case studies from the education and financial services sectors were presented. The financial analysis from my previous engagement with Worlducation was also shared with the team, demonstrating the unsustainability of the hardware-centric model and reinforcing the strategic rationale for the Cloud-First approach.

## Request for Approval - Collaboration Platform

To further support the team's cohesion and collaboration - particularly given that some team members are working remotely - I would like to request approval for the adoption of a dedicated collaboration platform.

Based on the team's assessment, **Microsoft Teams** is the recommended solution, as it:

- Integrates with our existing Microsoft environment
- Supports video conferencing, file sharing, and project communication in a single platform
- Is compliant with Australian data privacy requirements
- Reduces the information silos currently affecting team performance

I am available to discuss this report at your earliest convenience and would welcome your feedback on the proposed solutions.
